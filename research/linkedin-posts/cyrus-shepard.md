New — Fan-out Framework: 5 Steps to Improve SEO and AI Visibility

You already know about AI fan-out queries — the related sub-queries that AI generates to more fully answer a question. Ranking highly for multiple fan-out queries can increase your chances of getting cited or appearing in the AI answer.

But how do you optimize for AI query fan-outs? The process:

1. Use a keyword you already rank for
2. Find common fan-out queries
3. Determine the most important fan-out topics
4. Optimize your page — or create new pages — for the fan-out queries
5. Measure the results

I couldn't find a great guide on how to do all this, so I wrote one. Exact tools. Exact process.

Rant: Google says "good SEO is good GEO."

I don't disagree. But the same advice doesn't always work in reverse.

There are a whole lot of things AI-savvy SEOs do right now that they likely would never do if AI had never existed:

1. AI visibility tracking
2. Citation gap analysis
3. Building agent-ready sites
4. Optimizing for fan-out queries
5. Publishing more YouTube transcripts
6. Auditing AI crawler access + bot control
7. Greater emphasis on making facts explicit
8. Boosting entity consistency across the web
9. Those damn listicles

Are these good for SEO? Mostly, but not all. SEOs did many of the same things before AI, but not to the extent they do now.

Regardless, pay attention to new surfaces. And take Google's advice with a tiny grain of salt. They mostly want to sell us ads.

Most ranking declines in 2026 aren't about technical SEO, but the types of content Google now rewards.

So I built a "Competitive SEO Pattern Report" using Agent A.

Almost weekly, website owners reach out with the same problem — a decline in Google traffic. The problem is _almost never_ technical SEO — it's that Google simply rewards a different type of content now than "Commodity content + links."

The process I use:

1. Identify your winning and losing pages
2. Find competitors with the largest keyword overlap
3. Analyze URLs gaining and losing visibility across those sites
4. Classify pages by type: product, blog, comparison, research, how-to, definition, template, tool, etc.
5. Score the content against patterns we see working in Google SERPs — commodity vs. non-commodity, brand, experience, first-party info, freshness, task completion, format, etc.

The app uses live Ahrefs data to gather all metrics, find competitors, and evaluate content against predefined criteria.

> **Research note:** Ranking declines in 2026 are rarely technical — Google now rewards non-commodity content with brand, experience, first-party data, and task-completion signals. Shepard built an AI-powered Competitive SEO Pattern Report to identify which content type wins per vertical.
