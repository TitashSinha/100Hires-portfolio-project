"How would you start a marketing strategy from scratch?"

The annoying but true answer is: it depends. But I would start with one question:

1. How is this business supposed to grow?

Is the intended growth mechanism raw discovery? Then SEO, comparison pages, and educational content probably matter a lot.

Is it product-led growth? Then onboarding content, use-case content, templates, lifecycle emails, and activation loops probably matter more.

Is it sales-led? Then the first content priority might be the sales asset the team has been begging for, like a buyer guide or follow-up email sequence.

2. After this, look for the shortest path to proving marketing can help:

- Discovery-led motion → fix the highest-intent pages
- Sales-led motion → create the asset sales needs this month
- Product-led motion → improve the content that shortens time-to-value

Early on, marketing needs to earn internal trust. The fastest way to do that is to understand how the company grows, then make that motion work better.

Even when AI is wrong about you, you're still getting a useful data point.

When I ran SparkToro through an AI visibility tool, it compared us to Ahrefs, Semrush, Similarweb, Brandwatch, and others.

My first instinct: That's wrong. SparkToro isn't an SEO suite or enterprise social listening tool.

It's more useful to ask: _why_ did AI get it wrong?

Positioning isn't only what you say on your homepage. It's the context the broader market creates around you: what category you're in, what you replace, who you're for, and what buyers should compare you against. AI systems absorb that context from across the internet.

So don't let AI redefine your positioning. But don't dismiss its mistakes as mere hallucinations, either. Ask what might have produced the association.

AI visibility isn't just a score. It's a reflection of your public record.

"What's our AI visibility like?"

If my CEO or CMO asked me that, here's what I'd do.

First, run our brand through HubSpot's AEO Grader. Then summarize what stands out.

The report correctly identified SparkToro as a niche audience research tool and picked up on our biggest advantage: credibility with marketing practitioners.

Other parts need a human read: the competitive set was messy — SparkToro gets compared to Semrush, Ahrefs, Similarweb, Brandwatch. Some are fair nearby alternatives. But this info is useful — it shows how AI is categorizing the brand, even when it gets that wrong.

Here's what I'd do next:

1. Find what's accurate — what is AI already getting right?
2. Find the misconceptions — where is AI putting you in the wrong category?
3. Turn the gaps into priorities — that's a to-do list for website copy, content, PR, and category education.

Then run this analysis again in 1-2 months to see what moved.
