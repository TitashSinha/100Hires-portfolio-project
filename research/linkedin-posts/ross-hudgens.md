"If you use AI to evaluate us, you're likelier to buy."

That's HubSpot's single biggest predictor of purchase intent right now.

Aja Frost came on the podcast and opened up their category-leading GEO playbook. She shared several insights worth digging into:

First, HubSpot's primary KPI isn't citations. It's visibility — how often LLMs reference you and what they actually say. Citations drive some traffic, but most people never click them.

Second, they reorganized their 40-person team around products, not sections of the website. Every person owns the LLM visibility of a product and the demand it generates. The messy middle disappears when nobody's measured on their section's traffic anymore.

Third, their affiliate model now prices a citation directly. A new senior director built a methodology for what a single citation is worth to HubSpot, then plugged it into their existing affiliate bid strategy. Most programs still pay on last-click conversions and quietly undervalue the content shaping the LLM.

And a final line that stood out: B2B2C is becoming B2B2Bot. Your primary reader, especially in B2B, is often the agent.

## Post 2

**Date:** June 20, 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosshudgens_b2c-content-marketing-does-not-drive-many-activity-7472660098964959232-Hc4I

B2C content marketing does not drive many direct LLM clicks in 2026.

In our analysis of 61 brands' content-type traffic from LLMs, traffic to editorial sections for B2C brands was only 7%.

On the B2B side, it was a completely different story: editorial/blog sections were more than a quarter of the clicks.

The likely reason? In B2B, "BOFU content" is a thing. Alternative and "Best X" pages are more prolific and performative in a way that they aren't in B2C. Those conversion-focused pages get cited more in LLMs, all things equal, and thus are more likely to get clicks as a byproduct.

The strategy indication for B2C? Influence in LLMs comes from other factors like affiliate sites and product pages. Helping better optimize those pages, increasing the authority of your site, and driving mentions within affiliate listings is how you drive LLM influence instead.

## Post 3

**Date:** June 13, 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosshudgens_ai-traffic-converts-34-better-than-organic-activity-7470498245148934144-JQb4

AI traffic converts 34% better than organic on average.

In B2B, it's 49%. In B2C, a tamer 19%.

That's what we found reviewing 77 sites with statistically significant AI search traffic.

At the start of the LLM fervor, people were claiming AI converted 4x, 5x, even 7x better than traditional search. The wider majority sits in a much tighter band, closer to organic than the hype suggested. That's still impressive.

A big reason B2B converts so much better than B2C is bottom-funnel transactional content. X vs Y, Y alternatives, best X software. Those comparison types are simply more prevalent in B2B.

If your conversion rate lags and you don't have that content, this is one more justification to build it.

And if you think AI search is an underwhelming channel for you, it may just be that you don't have the content to support the traffic, so it never feels like one.
