## Post 1

**Date:** June 26, 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/schwartze_everyone-at-cannes-wanted-to-talk-about-ai-activity-7476254190856757248-iW0R

Everyone at Cannes wanted to talk about AI search, but very few are talking about what comes after it.

There were dozens of conversations about how AI is eating the SERP, how brands need to show up in AI answers, and how citations are the new backlinks. That fight is already underway.

There's a second wave coming, and it's going to hit a lot harder than the first one. Personalization. Not the watered down kind. Real personalization, where the AI answering a query already knows who's asking and shapes the response around that person specifically.

Marketing has spent two decades building campaigns around personas — a fictional 35-year-old VP of Marketing. That persona was always a compromise. AI search no longer needs to compromise because it can talk to the actual person in front of it.

A SaaS founder asking about software gets a completely different answer than an enterprise IT buyer asking the exact same question.

There is no average customer anymore in an AI-mediated world. There's just the customer, the actual one, sitting on the other side of the prompt.

> **Research note:** Schwartz predicts real personalization in AI search — the same query will yield different answers based on individual user context, breaking persona-based content strategies. Generic keyword-driven content stops working when AI matches users, not keywords.

## Post 2

**Date:** June 26, 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/schwartze_after-spending-the-last-few-days-in-cannes-activity-7475930759703478272-y1dF

After spending the last few days in Cannes Lions, one huge thing stood out. AI is going to eat huge parts of marketing, not someday but already.

Writing teams, media buyers, people who used to spend a week building a campaign brief, SEO, analytics, design, paid media, even video — that work is collapsing into a prompt and a few hours of human judgment layered on top.

But what survives, and actually grows, is influence. Trust transfers from a person, not a model. You can automate the brief but you can't automate why someone follows a specific human and believes what they say.

B2B is sitting on this completely untapped. Consumer brands have spent years building creator economies, agencies, payment rails, the whole infrastructure. B2B has none of it. Most B2B "influencer marketing" is still just a sponsored LinkedIn post and a hope.

> **Research note:** Technical marketing tasks are collapsing into prompts, but human influence and trust remain irreplaceable. B2B brands have a major untapped opportunity in building creator-like infrastructure around human experts.

## Post 3

**Date:** June 23, 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/schwartze_ai-search-is-a-zero-click-channel-and-your-activity-7474823020495327302-FRjs

AI search is a zero-click channel, and your dashboard is built to miss it.

When any LLM recommends a brand to someone who's never searched for it, that person becomes 182% more likely to Google the brand within a week. 117% more likely to visit the site. Almost 200% more likely to land on a product page.

None of that shows up as "AI traffic" — it shows up as organic or direct. Another team takes the credit and never asks where the demand actually came from.

A mention isn't a recommendation. AI actively putting your brand forward does roughly twice the work at every step. Get named first, and you're 389% more likely to get searched. Get framed as "best" or "top," and that number jumps past 400%.

I've been saying for months that AEO is a brand problem dressed up as a technical one. This is the data that proves it. You don't win a recommendation with a schema tag.

> **Research note:** Data shows AI brand mentions make users 182% more likely to search for the brand within a week — but this shows up as organic or direct in GA, not AI referral. AEO is a brand authority problem, not a technical one.
