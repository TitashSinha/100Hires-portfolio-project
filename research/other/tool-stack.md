> This document compiles every tool, platform, and resource mentioned by practitioners across 25 sources. ## 1. AI Visibility and GEO Tracking

**HubSpot AEO Grader**

Free tool that scores a brand's visibility across major AI platforms — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and others. Returns a share-of-voice breakdown, competitive set identified by AI, and positioning gaps. Amanda Natividad used it to audit SparkToro's AI representation and turned the output into a content and PR priority list.

_Mentioned by:_ Amanda Natividad, Ross Hudgens
_Use case:_ Baseline AI visibility audit; identifying how AI categorizes your brand vs. competitors

**Bing Webmaster Tools — AI Performance Report**

Provides grounding query data, citation share, intent classification, and topic clustering for how your site is being used in Bing's AI search. The only major search platform currently providing structured AI citation data directly to publishers. Newly released features (June 2026): Intents, Topics, Citation Share, and Compare.

_Mentioned by:_ Aleyda Solis, Chima Mmeje
_Use case:_ Tracking AI citation share; understanding which queries trigger your site as a grounding source

**Google Search Console — Branded Search Lift**

Not an AI-specific tool, but practitioners use branded impression growth in GSC as a proxy for AI mention activity — since LLM recommendations often manifest as branded searches rather than direct referral clicks.

_Mentioned by:_ Chima Mmeje, Aleyda Solis, Ross Hudgens
_Use case:_ Indirect measurement of AI visibility impact on brand demand

**Zyppy**

SEO tool built by Cyrus Shepard with a focus on on-page optimization signals. Used alongside AI visibility tracking for content scoring and optimization recommendations.

_Mentioned by:_ Cyrus Shepard
_Use case:_ On-page SEO auditing and content optimization

## 2. AI Content and Research Tools

**Claude (Anthropic)**

Used across sources for content drafting, editorial QA, prompt engineering, and workflow automation. Ryan Law uses it at Ahrefs for automated content update pipelines. Chima Mmeje built an internal linking workflow using Claude conversationally — without code or agents.

_Mentioned by:_ Ryan Law, Chima Mmeje, Bernard Huang, multiple YouTube sources
_Use case:_ Content drafting, editorial QA, internal linking, workflow automation, reasoning chain analysis

**ChatGPT (OpenAI)**

Used for content ideation, research synthesis, and as a grounding test environment — practitioners run their brand name through ChatGPT to see how it is described and what competitive set it is placed in.

_Mentioned by:_ Amanda Natividad, Bernard Huang, Nathan Gotch, multiple sources
_Use case:_ Brand visibility auditing, content ideation, competitive positioning research

**Perplexity**

Used for research synthesis and as a test platform for AI search visibility. Also flagged in Aleyda Solis's sources as unreliable in self-reporting — Perplexity told a user it "couldn't access" a page while its own crawler had already fetched it.

_Mentioned by:_ Aleyda Solis, multiple sources
_Use case:_ Research synthesis, AI visibility testing; note unreliability in self-reported crawler behavior

**Gemini (Google)**

Used as a visibility test environment — checking how Google's AI represents a brand. SparkToro measured a 12.5% share of voice in Gemini vs. 24% in ChatGPT/Perplexity, revealing platform-specific positioning gaps.

_Mentioned by:_ Amanda Natividad, multiple sources
_Use case:_ Platform-specific AI visibility testing

**Google AI Studio**

Used by Bernard Huang for reasoning chain analysis — prompting a topic, folding down the thinking component, and identifying the sub-themes AI uses in its research before generating a response. Advanced use case for understanding how AI builds its answer.

_Mentioned by:_ Bernard Huang
_Use case:_ Reasoning chain analysis; identifying AI fan-out sub-queries before creating content

**Cursor**

AI-powered code editor used for building content automation workflows and tools.

_Mentioned by:_ Multiple practitioner sources
_Use case:_ Building AI-assisted content production tools and automation scripts

## 5. Measurement and Attribution

**Self-Attribution Survey / "How Did You Hear About Us?" Field**

Not a tool but a measurement practice — adding a free-text or dropdown field to conversion forms asking users how they discovered the brand. Recommended by multiple practitioners as the only reliable method for capturing AI-referred conversions that appear as direct traffic in analytics.

_Mentioned by:_ Ross Hudgens, Eli Schwartz, Aleyda Solis, Amanda Natividad
_Use case:_ Capturing AI search attribution that standard analytics platforms miss

**MCP Server (Model Context Protocol)**

Used by advanced practitioners to connect analytics data directly to AI tools for querying. Eli Schwartz references setting up an MCP server to measure lift from specific SEO changes by querying historical analytics data conversationally.

_Mentioned by:_ Eli Schwartz
_Use case:_ Advanced AI-assisted analytics querying; measuring SEO change impact

**Google Analytics 4**

Standard analytics platform. Practitioners flag a critical limitation: AI referrals frequently appear as direct or organic traffic in GA4, making AI impact invisible without supplementary measurement methods.

_Mentioned by:_ Ross Hudgens, Aleyda Solis, multiple sources
_Use case:_ Traffic analytics; note that AI referrals are systematically undercounted

**Scrunch**

AI search analytics platform. Eli Schwartz cited a Scrunch study at Cannes Lions showing that LLM brand recommendations increase branded Google search by 182% within a week — one of the most cited data points in the research.

_Mentioned by:_ Eli Schwartz
_Use case:_ AI search impact measurement; brand recommendation tracking

## 7. Technical SEO and Site Infrastructure

**LLMs.txt**

Proposed file standard (analogous to robots.txt) for providing AI crawlers with structured guidance about site content. Contested in terms of current adoption — some practitioners see no evidence it is being used; others recommend implementing it as a low-cost future-proofing measure.

_Mentioned by:_ Lily Ray, multiple sources
_Use case:_ Signaling content permissions and context to AI crawlers; agentic search preparation

**Schema Markup / Structured Data**

Recommended for clarifying the meaning and relationships of data on a page — particularly for product, pricing, availability, and entity data. Noted as necessary but not sufficient: schema helps AI understand content but does not substitute for genuine authority.

_Mentioned by:_ Chima Mmeje, Bernard Huang, Eli Schwartz
_Use case:_ Content meaning clarification for AI crawlers; agentic search optimization

**JavaScript Rendering Audit**

Critical technical check: US AI assistants (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Copilot) read raw HTML only — they do not render JavaScript. Content injected via JavaScript is effectively invisible to these crawlers. Aleyda Solis's sources flag this as one of the most commonly overlooked technical issues in AI search optimization.

_Mentioned by:_ Aleyda Solis
_Use case:_ Ensuring critical content is in raw HTML and accessible to AI crawlers

**Google Business Profile**

For local businesses, the GBP is identified as the primary driver of AI visibility in local queries — more important than website SEO. Review volume and profile optimization directly influence AI recommendation frequency.

_Mentioned by:_ Nathan Gotch
_Use case:_ Local AI search visibility; review volume as primary ranking signal
